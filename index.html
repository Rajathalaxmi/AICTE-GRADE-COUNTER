About AICTE

AICTE Accreditation, Approval, and Grading

AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education) mandates that all engineering students complete 100 Activity Points during their 4-year degree program. This system promotes holistic development beyond academics, focusing on practical skills, social responsibility, and community engagement. These points are reflected in the 8th semester grade card and do not affect SGPA/CGPA.

AICTE 100-Point Activity System

  • Mandatory Requirement: All engineering students must earn 100 activity points
  • Activity Categories:
    • Community Service: Rural development, social awareness campaigns
    • Technical Activities: Internships, workshops, technical competitions
    • Digital Literacy: Promoting digital transactions, IT awareness
    • Environmental: Water management, energy conservation, waste management
    • Entrepreneurship: Start-up activities, business development
    • Sports & Cultural: Participation in sports, cultural events
  • Point Allocation: Activities range from 20-90 points based on duration and impact
  • Flexible Timing: Can be completed over weekends and holidays throughout the course

Benefits of Activity Points

  • Holistic Development: Develops technical, social, and leadership skills
  • Industry Readiness: Practical experience through internships and projects
  • Social Responsibility: Encourages community service and social awareness
  • Career Enhancement: Demonstrates well-rounded personality to employers

Implementation & Tracking

  • Documentation: All activities must be properly documented and verified
  • Evaluation: Activities are assessed by designated activity heads
  • Grade Card Reflection: Points appear on final semester transcripts
  • Institutional Support: Colleges facilitate and coordinate activity opportunities
